question :the equation of the perpendicular bisector of$\vec{BC}$ is

$ (\vec{x}-\vec{\frac{B+C}{2}})(\vec{B}-\vec{C})=0$

Substitute numerical values and find the equations of the perpendicular bisectors of $ \vec{AB}$,$\vec{BC}$ and $\vec{CA}$.
\solution:
as given in the question
\begin{align}
\vec{A}&=\myvec{1 \\ -1}\\
 \vec{B}&=\myvec{-4 \\ 6}\\
  \vec{C}&=\myvec{-3 \\ -5}
  \end{align}
\begin{enumerate}
\item the equation of perpendicular bisector of $\vec{AB}$ is :
 \begin{align}
 \brak{\vec{x}-\vec{\frac{B+A}{2}}}\brak{\vec{A}-\vec{B}}&=0\\
 \vec{\frac{B+A}{2}}&=\myvec{\frac{-3}{2} \\ \frac{5}{2}}\\
 \vec{A}-\vec{B}&=\myvec{5 \\ -7}\\
 \brak{\vec{x}-\myvec{\frac{-3}{2} \\ \frac{5}{2}}}\myvec{5 \\ -7}&=0
 \end{align}
\item  the equation of perpendicular bisector of $\vec{BC}$ is :
 \begin{align}
 \brak{\vec{x}-\vec{\frac{B+C}{2}}}\brak{\vec{B}-\vec{C}}&=0\\
 \vec{\frac{B+C}{2}}&=\myvec{\frac{-7}{2} \\ \frac{1}{2}}\\
 \vec{B}-\vec{C}&=\myvec{-1 \\ 11}\\
 \brak{\vec{x}-\myvec{\frac{-7}{2} \\  \frac{1}{2}}}\myvec{-1 \\ 11}&=0
 \end{align}
 \item  the equation of perpendicular bisector of $\vec{CA}$ is :
 \begin{align}
 \brak{\vec{x}-\vec{\frac{A+C}{2}}}\brak{\vec{C}-\vec{A}}&=0\\
 \vec{\frac{A+C}{2}}&=\myvec{\frac{-1}{2} \\ -3}\\
 \vec{C}-\vec{A}&=\myvec{-4 \\ -4}\\
\brak{\vec{x}-\myvec{-1 \\ -3}}\myvec{-4 \\ -4}&=0
\end{align}
\end{enumerate}
